Our Mission

        The City Hoops Institute utilizes basketball to promote development and peace among youth, young adults, and communities by ensuring developmental assets are available to enhance life skills, core competencies, and whole potential. 

Information

The City Hoops Institute provides much more beyond basketball resources. We believe a wide range of academic, athletic, health, and wellness consulting services and resources should be available to all individuals, organizations, and communities of all ages and types to help them achieve their whole potential in multiple environments of society.

Utilizing aspects of basketball, other sports, and various activities to transfer life skills, the CHI specializes in individual, organizational, & community development by ensuring access to essential internal and external assets.

Positive Development

The City Hoops Institute focuses on positive development to build life skills through sport, for students, athletes, and others to achieve their whole potential, but to also ease community tension, to decrease conflict and to enhance decision making abilities and other core competencies in multiple environments, such as before or after school, at home, or in the community.

Coaches

Brandon Grissette

Brandon Grissette is a 2007 Elgin High School Graduate, a 3 year varsity starter, and the 17th all-time leading scorer at Elgin High School (1,026 points). Brandon was a 2 time Daily Herald All-area player and an All-Conference Honorable Mention.

After high school, Brandon continued playing collegiately at Kennedy King College, where he made the Region IV District playoff all tournament team, and Bellevue University, where he led his team to a Division II NAIA Sweet 16 appearance.

Brandon then played one season with the Libertyville Vipers of the American Basketball Association until he became an assistant basketball coach and the Deanâ€™s Assistant at his alma mater, Elgin HS. Brandon also acts as a youth coordinator with the Boys and Girls Club of Elgin.

Jeremy Granger

Jeremy Granger played for head coach Mike Sitter at Elgin High School, he was named fourth team All-State by Illinois High School Basketball Coaches Association and participated in the IBCA All-Star game. Jeremy was a two-time first team All-Upstate Eight Conference selection and two-time All-Area by the Daily Herald. Jeremy averaged 17 points, 5.2 rebounds, 3.5 assists and 2.0 steals as a senior leading Elgin HS to a 21-10 record and appearance in the IHSA Class 4A Elite Eight, he ranked 8th all-time at Elgin HS with 1,115 career points and advanced to IHSA State Finals for the 3-Point Showdown.

Jeremy continued playing after HS in 118 games with 89 starts at Eastern Illinois University. Jeremy finished third in career games played at EIU, finished with 1,287 career points, is their career leader in free throw percentage at 86.9, is ranked fifth in career free throws made and third in career assists with 313.

After graduating in 2012, Jeremy went on to play professionally overseas for 4 years, in Turkey, Greece, Mexico, and Slovakia.
